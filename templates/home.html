Types Of Mental Disorders

Depression

It is characterized by sadness, loss of interest, guilt or low self-esteem, disturbed sleep or appetite, tiredness, and poor concentration. People with depression may also experience multiple physical complaints without an apparent cause. Long-term or recurrent depression can significantly impair people's ability to function at work or school and cope with daily life. Severe depression can even lead to suicide.

Bipolar disorder

A bipolar episode consists of both manic and depressive episodes separated by normal periods. Manic episodes are characterized by elevated moods, overactivity, rapid speech and inflated self-esteem, as well as a decreased need for sleep. Those who have manic episodes but no depressive episodes are also considered to have bipolar disorder.

Schizophrenia

The symptoms of schizophrenia include distortions in thinking, perception, emotions, language, sense of self, and behavior. Psychotic experiences include hallucinations (hearing, seeing, or feeling things that aren't there) and delusions (fixed false beliefs or suspicions firmly held despite contrary evidence). It can make it difficult for people affected to work or study normally.Schizophrenia typically begins in late adolescence or early adulthood. Medications and psychosocial support are effective treatments.

Dementia

The disorder is usually chronic or progressive in nature and causes deterioration of cognitive function (i.e. the ability to process thoughts) beyond that which is expected from normal aging. Memory, thinking, orientation, comprehension, calculation, learning capacity, language, and judgement are all affected by it. Cognitive impairment is frequently accompanied, or may even precede, deterioration in emotional control, social behavior, or motivation.

alzheimer

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurologic condition that causes the brain to shrink (atrophy) and its cells to die. Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia - a progressive decline in cognitive, behavioral, and social skills that affects a person's ability to function independently.

Post-traumatic stress disorder

A recurring trauma: memories, nightmares, or flashbacks Feeling "numb" Avoiding certain people or places Intense emotions, such as anger, fear, or worry Trouble sleeping (insomnia)
